MAC STUDIO UNDER-DESK ENCLOSURE — DESIGN SPECIFICATION
========================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
A hollow, open-back enclosure sized to hold a Mac Studio on its side.
The Mac Studio slides in from the back. The enclosure mounts under a
desk via screw slots on two opposing faces.

All measurements below are derived from the Mac Studio's own body.
Start by measuring your specific model, then follow the formulas.


REFERENCE MEASUREMENTS TO TAKE FROM YOUR MAC STUDIO
----------------------------------------------------
Measure the following from your Mac Studio with calipers:

  A.  Body width (square footprint, flat edge to flat edge)
  B.  Body height (top face to bottom face)
  C.  Body corner radius (the rounded vertical edges)
  D.  Front panel port area width
  E.  Front panel port area height
  F.  Front panel port area corner radius (if rounded)
  G.  Bottom base circle diameter (the raised or recessed ring
      on the underside — this is the ventilation intake zone)
  H.  Bottom base circle protrusion depth (how far the base
      extends below the main body, if at all)


MAIN BODY
---------
The enclosure is a cuboid shell that wraps the Mac Studio with
clearance on all sides.

  Enclosure width  =  A + 11 mm  (roughly 5.5 mm clearance per side)
  Enclosure depth  =  A + 11 mm  (same, since the footprint is square)
  Enclosure height =  B + 5 mm   (roughly 2.5 mm clearance top and bottom)

The clearance serves two purposes: it accounts for manufacturing
tolerance in the print, and it leaves a small air gap around the
Mac Studio for passive airflow.

All four long edges of the cuboid are rounded. The enclosure corner
radius should match the Mac Studio's own corner profile plus the
same clearance:

  Enclosure corner radius  =  C + 5.5 mm

This keeps the air gap uniform around the entire perimeter rather
than pinching at the corners.

The enclosure is hollow with a uniform wall thickness of 4 mm. This
is not derived from the Mac Studio — it is a structural choice. At
4 mm, most FDM slicers will fill the wall solidly with 3-4
perimeters, producing a rigid shell without relying on infill.

The inner corner radius follows from the outer:

  Inner corner radius  =  Enclosure corner radius - 4 mm


FACE DEFINITIONS
----------------
Orient the model so the front face (where the Mac Studio's front
ports are) faces you:

  Front:   Width x Height  (display/port cutout)
  Back:    Width x Height  (entirely open)
  Left:    Depth x Height  (screw slots)
  Right:   Depth x Height  (screw slots)
  Top:     Width x Depth   (square cutout)
  Bottom:  Width x Depth   (circular cutout and channel)


BACK — OPEN
------------
The entire back face is removed. The Mac Studio slides in from this
side, and all rear ports and the power cable remain accessible.


FRONT — PORT/DISPLAY CUTOUT
----------------------------
A rectangular cutout on the front face, centered both horizontally
and vertically, passing fully through the 4 mm wall.

In the current design, the front opening is intentionally wider than
the earlier conservative "maximum access" version. A printed fit test
and orthographic render overlay showed that the outer front USB-C port
sits low enough on the Mac Studio front panel that a large lower corner
radius eats into the usable width exactly where that port lives.

The implemented fix keeps the cutout centered, increases its width,
reduces its corner radius, and adds a light chamfer on the exterior
edge of the wall:

  Cutout width   =  Enclosure width
                   - 2 x enclosure corner radius
                   - 2 x front-width margin
                   + 12 mm extra width

  Cutout height  =  Enclosure height - 2 x front-height margin

With the current Mac Studio-derived numbers in this repo, that yields:

  Cutout width   ≈ 138.2 mm
  Cutout height  =  70 mm

All four corners of this cutout are rounded with a small radius:

  Cutout corner radius  =  3 mm

The smaller radius keeps more usable width near the bottom port band
than a 12-13 mm aesthetic radius would.

The outer edge of the front wall is chamfered around the opening:

  Front cutout chamfer  =  1.5 mm

This chamfer is a comfort and finish detail on the outside surface; it
does not reduce the nominal through-opening deeper in the wall.


TOP — SQUARE CUTOUT
--------------------
A square cutout centered on the top face, passing fully through the
4 mm wall. This saves material and can aid ventilation.

The current design ties the top opening directly to the front opening
width for visual consistency:

  Top cutout side  =  Front cutout width

With the current values in this repo:

  Top cutout side  ≈ 138.2 mm

This is larger than the earlier 130 mm recommendation, but it still
leaves roughly 35 mm of material between the cutout and the outer
edge on each flat side of the 208 mm top face.

The top cutout uses the same edge treatment as the front opening:

  Top cutout corner radius  =  3 mm
  Top cutout chamfer        =  1.5 mm

As on the front face, the chamfer is applied only to the exterior
edge of the wall opening.


BOTTOM — CIRCULAR CUTOUT AND CHANNEL
-------------------------------------
A circular cutout centered on the bottom face, passing fully through
the 4 mm wall. This aligns with and exposes the Mac Studio's bottom
ventilation intake.

  Circle diameter  =  G  (the diameter of the base ring/vent circle
    on the Mac Studio's underside)

If your Mac Studio's base protrudes below the main body (measurement
H), the enclosure also needs a channel so the base can slide in from
the back. This channel runs from the widest point of the circle
straight to the back edge of the bottom face:

  Channel width  =  G  (same as the circle diameter)
  Channel length =  from the center of the circle to the back edge,
                    i.e. half the enclosure depth

The resulting shape on the bottom face is a circle with a rectangular
tongue extending from its equator to the rear edge. If your Mac
Studio model has a flush base with no protrusion, you may omit the
channel, but keeping it does no structural harm and ensures
compatibility with other Mac Studio models.


LEFT AND RIGHT SIDES — SCREW SLOTS
-----------------------------------
Each side face has four slotted holes for mounting hardware. The
pattern is identical on both sides.

These are not derived from the Mac Studio. They are a mounting
design based on standard hardware:

Slot dimensions:
  Each slot is a stadium (oblong) shape, 10 mm long and 5 mm wide.
  The 5 mm width provides clearance for M5 bolts. The 10 mm length
  provides adjustment travel for alignment during installation.

  If you plan to use a different bolt size, set the slot width to
  your bolt shaft diameter + 0.5 mm clearance, and scale the slot
  length proportionally.

Slot arrangement:
  The four slots form a rectangle on each side face:

    Rectangle width   =  50 mm  (along the depth axis)
    Rectangle height  =  30 mm  (along the height axis)

  The rectangle is centered along the depth of the face. The top
  edge is 14 mm from the top of the enclosure. This places the
  rectangle's center at:

    29 mm from the top edge
    Centered left-to-right on the face

  The 14 mm top offset keeps the upper slots close to the desk
  surface for a tight, stable mount. The 30 x 50 mm spread gives
  a wide enough base to resist rotation.

  Slot center positions (measured from the top-front corner of the
  side face):

    Top-front:      79 mm from front,  14 mm from top
    Top-back:      129 mm from front,  14 mm from top
    Bottom-front:   79 mm from front,  44 mm from top
    Bottom-back:   129 mm from front,  44 mm from top

Slot orientation:
  Each slot's long axis is angled to point toward the center of the
  50 x 30 mm rectangle. This means the four slots converge radially,
  so that tightening the bolts tends to pull the enclosure toward a
  centered position on the mounting bracket.

  The angle for each slot, measured as rotation from vertical:

    Top-front:     ≈ 59° clockwise from vertical
    Top-back:      ≈ 59° counter-clockwise from vertical
    Bottom-front:  ≈ 59° counter-clockwise from vertical
    Bottom-back:   ≈ 59° clockwise from vertical

  These angles come from the aspect ratio of the rectangle:
  atan2(25, 15) ≈ 59.0°, since each slot is 25 mm horizontally
  and 15 mm vertically from the center.


MOUNTING ORIENTATION
--------------------
In use, the enclosure hangs under a desk. One screw-slot face is
fastened against the underside of the desk (or a mounting bracket
attached to the desk). The opposite screw-slot face faces the floor.

The Mac Studio sits on its side inside the enclosure. The open back
faces outward for cable access. The front port/display cutout faces
inward (toward the user's seated position) or outward, depending on
preference.


SUMMARY OF WHAT COMES FROM THE MAC STUDIO
------------------------------------------
  Enclosure width/depth    →  Mac Studio footprint + 11 mm
  Enclosure height         →  Mac Studio height + 5 mm
  Enclosure corner radius  →  Mac Studio corner radius + 5.5 mm
  Front cutout size        →  Structural/access choice informed by
                              the front port layout; current
                              implementation is a widened centered cutout
  Front cutout radius      →  Structural/access choice; current
                              implementation uses 3 mm
  Front cutout chamfer     →  Exterior finish/detail choice;
                              current implementation uses 1.5 mm
  Bottom circle diameter   →  Mac Studio base/vent ring diameter
  Bottom channel           →  Exists if Mac Studio base protrudes

  Wall thickness (4 mm)    →  Structural choice, not from Mac Studio
  Top cutout (~138.2 mm)   →  Structural/visual choice; current
                              implementation matches front cutout width
  Top cutout chamfer       →  Exterior finish/detail choice;
                              current implementation uses 1.5 mm
  Screw slot pattern       →  Mounting choice, based on M5 hardware
